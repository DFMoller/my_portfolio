Porche's new Brake Rotors

July 27, Monday

There have been many examples of people attempting to "reinvent the wheel" just before realizing that the wheel is round for a reason. However, Porsche's attempt to rethink the common brake rotor has proven quite successful thus far. These rotors are coated with a thin layer of thungsten carbide which gives it an extremely hard finish. This means there is less brake fade and less brake dust than carbon ceramic rotors have and they last up to 30% longer than conventional iron brake rotors.

How safe is a Sawstop?

July 13, Monday

If you're anything like me and have tried doing some woodwork at some point using only the most basic of tools, you'll have heard of a table saw, but never used one. Similarly, I've heard of the countless injuries people have suffered using these. However, some of the more advanced table saws have recently become much more safe. A tablesaw like this uses a sawstop system that can detect when human flesh comes into contact with the saw teeth and stops the blade instantly. This is a very interesting watch.

Crew Dragon Rocket Launch Day

May 25, Monday

Crew Dragon is the capsule SpaceX intends use to send two astronauts to the international space station (ISS) on 27 May 2020. It carries some significance as it will be the first time a private company (SpaceX) takes people so the ISS. It will also be the first time astronauts are launched from American soil this decade, but its really only american patriots that care about that.
This video gives a step-by-step breakdown of the entire launch day up to the point where Crew Dragon docks the ISS.
